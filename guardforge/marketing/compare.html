When GuardForge is NOT the right choice

You need ML-based NER over unstructured text

GuardForge is regex + heuristics. If you have unstructured free text where names rarely have titles ("Mr"/"Dr"/"Mme") and you need high recall on person names, Presidio with spaCy or Private AI's transformer NER will outperform us. We're on the roadmap for optional spaCy integration.

You need hundreds of entity types out of the box

Our 17 built-in entities cover 95% of GDPR/HIPAA/PCI use cases. But if you need to detect things like drug names, ICD codes, medical device serial numbers, etc., Presidio ships with more and Nightfall has specialized detectors. You can always add them via our custom entities API — but it's manual work.

You need SOC 2 Type II today

SOC 2 Type I is planned for 2026 H2, Type II for 2027 H1. If your procurement team requires certified vendors NOW, Nightfall and Private AI are your only options — and they charge accordingly. Self-hosted GuardForge lets you inherit your own certifications.

You need a large sales team to hand-hold enterprise rollouts

GuardForge is a self-serve product with community, email, and (at higher tiers) Slack support. If you need a dedicated Customer Success Manager on week one, our Enterprise tier (999€+) offers this — but the big players offer more mature CSM programs at 3-5x the price.

When GuardForge IS the right choice

  • EU-headquartered companies worried about EU AI Act 2024/1689 entering force — we have the preset built in, before anyone else.
  • SaaS companies adding AI features that need PII redaction without a 6-figure annual budget.
  • Self-hosted requirements (data residency, air-gapped networks, regulated industries) with one-time licensing instead of per-seat enterprise contracts.
  • Multi-language products — 15-language dashboard out of the box.
  • Developers who want a real SDK, not a proxy endpoint. `from guardforge import OpenAI` just works.
  • Teams that care about compliance reporting — our PDF export is GDPR Article 30 ready, not a raw CSV dump.

Honest limitations

We publish all our known limitations in our public limitations doc, including:

  • Vault persistence requires SQLite (PostgreSQL falls back to in-memory mode)
  • Streaming LLM responses are not yet detokenized (workaround: buffer then detokenize)
  • Async clients (AsyncOpenAI, AsyncAnthropic) are not yet wrapped
  • Single-tenant by default (multi-tenant on roadmap for v0.3)
  • Rate limiting is per-process (use Redis or a reverse proxy for multi-node)

Most competitors do not publish their limitations publicly. We do.
